# Exploring the Distinctions Between Two Final Stages in Alzheimer's Disease

**Summer 2024 SoCal Data Science — Group 5**
**Authors: Julia Eng, Kent Tran, Olivia Willard, Ted Yoon, Wesley Zeng, Yen Phuong Lam, Yvette Vargas**

Using patient data spanning multiple cognition levels, this study seeks to differentiate between individuals with Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) and those with Alzheimer's Disease (AD) through predictive modeling. Rather than relying on expensive or complex diagnostic tools such as MRI scans and biomarkers, the research draws on more accessible sources — including neuropsychological assessments, depression and behavioral questionnaires, and demographic data — to build reliable, practical diagnostic models.

The ultimate goal is to improve early detection and diagnostic accuracy for both MCI and Alzheimer's Disease, supporting better patient outcomes and more effective management of this serious condition.
